About Audio

Audio can be incorporated into your movies in various ways.

See the Movie

tasks associated with audio
  • Add Audio to a Movie After Recording You can apply audio to an entire movie after you finish recording it. This includes the ability to select built-in system sounds, recorded audio, or imported files (such as MP3 and WAV files). For example, you might use this feature to play music in the background of a movie. See Adding Audio to Movies.
  • Add Audio (e.g., Narration) to a Movie During Recording You can apply audio to an entire movie while you are recording it. This typically involves attaching a microphone to your computer (if necessary) and narrating while you record your actions on the screen. See Recording Movies and Selecting the Audio Input Source.
  • Add Audio to Individual Frames You can apply audio to individual frames in a movie. For example, you might use this feature to play recorded narration in order to help explain an action on a frame. See Adding Audio to Frames.
  • Add Audio to Cursors You can apply audio to cursors. For example, you can apply a double-click sound to a cursor to replace the single-click sound. See Adding Audio to Objects.
  • Add Audio as Objects in a Frame You can apply audio as objects in a frame. See Adding Audio as Objects.
  • Record Audio You can easily record audio, such as narration, for your Mimic movies. The recorded audio file can be created for an individual frame, a cursor, or an entire movie. When you record audio in Mimic, it is stored as an MP3 file in a folder called "Sounds" where your movie files are located. For cursors, the audio file plays when the cursor reaches the end of its trajectory. See Recording Audio.
  • Loop Audio You can loop any audio file, causing it to restart when it reaches the end of the playing time. This is useful, for example, if you want to play background music for the entire duration of a frame. You can loop audio for a single frame and cursors. See Looping Audio.
  • Edit Audio Files After you record an audio file, you can edit the file in several different ways, such as adjusting the volume, deleting sections, and inserting silent areas. See Editing Audio Files.
  • Enable or Disable Sounds When You Record Movies If this feature is enabled, normal sounds (such as mouse clicks) can be heard as you record a movie. See Enabling and Disabling Sound for Recording.
