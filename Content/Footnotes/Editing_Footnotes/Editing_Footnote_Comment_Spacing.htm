Editing Footnote Comment Spacing

 

When using styles, you can do so in either the Advanced or Simplified view in the Stylesheet Editor. However, most settings can be applied only by using the Advanced view.

Otherwise, if you have used a class of that style (e.g., MadCap|footnote.BeginnersManual) that you created previously, then select it instead.

  6. Do any of the following, depending on what you want to accomplish.

    add space (indentation) in front of comment number and text
    1. If you are using the group view, expand the Box (for indenting all lines in the comment) or Block (for indenting the first line in the comment) group.
    2. Locate the margin-left property in the "Box" group (for indenting all lines in the comment), or locate the text-indent property in the "Block" group (for indenting the first line in the comment).
    3. Click in the value column on the right side of that property. In the popup, enter a number and a unit of measurement, and click OK.

    3. Click in the value column on the right side of the appropriate property. In the popup, enter a number and a unit of measurement, and click OK.
         adjust space between characters or words in comment
    1.  
    2. Locate the letter-spacing and word-spacing properties (for setting the space between characters or words, respectively).
    3. Click in the value column on the right side of the appropriate property. In the popup, enter a number and a unit of measurement, and click OK.
