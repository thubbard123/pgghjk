Concept Window Pane

The Concept window pane is split into two sections. The top portion is the concept entry area and is used to type new concepts to be inserted wherever the cursor rests in the content file. The bottom portion is the explorer area and can be used for a variety of purposes, including viewing, creating, and assigning concepts. See About Concepts.

how to open the concepts window pane

  • Ribbon In the Reference section select Concept Window.
  • Keyboard Shortcut Press Shift+F9.

  • Ribbon In the Index and Concepts section select Concept Window.
  • Keyboard Shortcut Press Shift+F9.
 what you can do in the concept window pane

Following are the primary tasks that you can perform in this window pane.

  • Create Concepts There are two ways to link a topic to a concept using this window pane. First, you can open the topic, place your cursor where you want to insert the concept, and use the top area of the Concept window pane to create the keyword (see Inserting Concepts). Second, you can also use the bottom area of the Concept window pane to add concepts (see Adding Concepts) and assign topics to them (see Assigning Topics to Concepts).
  • Remove You can use either the top or bottom area of the window pane to remove concepts. The difference is that deleting a concept from the top section removes that concept from the active topic only. On the other hand, deleting a concept from the bottom section removes that concept from all topics where it is inserted. See Deleting Concepts.
  • Rename You can use either the top or bottom area of the window pane to create concepts. The difference is that renaming a concept in the top section renames that concept in the active topic only. On the other hand, renaming a concept in the bottom section renames that concept in all topics where it is inserted. See Editing Concepts.
