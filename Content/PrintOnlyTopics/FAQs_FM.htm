Why Does the User Interface Look So Different from Adobe FrameMaker?

why does the user interface look so different from adobe framemaker?

There are significant differences between the and user interfaces.

In , a window appears when you open a document. The window shows the document laid out in a page design with text, images, and other elements in their places. You can customize the interface slightly by displaying window guides such as borders, text symbols, rulers, and grids.

was developed in the late 1970s, while is much more modern looking and is designed to support the future of single-source publishing.

Unlike the interface, which presents a traditional page analog for text entry with pull-down menus for additional options, features a visual XML editor, driven by cascading stylesheets.

Editors are available for every project element and can be arranged as tabs or accordion items; docked or floating. You can open and work in multiple windows to suit your preferences. Visual indicators of conditions, variables, and index markers speed your development process.
